About Me

My name is Brent and I am a game developer. I mainly program in C++ and C#(Unity) and use the Unity engine and a bootstrap provided by my college to develop games. I really enjoy programming because it is like a big book that can always be republished with new and rewritten information and techniques. There is always a problem or mountain to overcome, and learning to solve those issues are quite an enjoyable thing to do, and when I solve it, it feels great knowing that I am able to easily fix or avoid the problem next time. I would love to either work as a gameplay engineer or engine engineer one day as a lead developer, or even run my own company with the knowledge gained through my career.

I have a passion for games and being able to code them myself is really a dream come true. I am a honest and straightforward guy with motivation that drives me to succeed and conquer those problems thrown at me. I love facing new problems and completing tasks as I know that at the end of that journey I will have gained some knowledge. I put family first before anything in life as they are very important to me and they help drive me to succeed. I always strive to do my best and please others in a respectable and honest manner. I always look to the road ahead with a positive manner..

Labyrinthine

This was my major project at the end of 2017 where we spent six weeks in development to create our own version of Thumper. It is set in a post apocalyptic world where the goal is to reach the city and detonate the fuel cell on your car. The project was created by three designers, three artists and two programmers. My work on the project was creating tools for the designers to easily create and place obstacles on the road and save that to the real scene. I also worked on the player movement and effects that the obstacles apply onto the player.
Engine: Unity
Designers: Devon Sanders, Keith Rogers and Jake Reason
Artists: Jasper Lepani, Raphael Buenaventura and Kim Niemann
Programmers: Brent Kingma and Mark Sturtz
